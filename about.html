What is Quantum Computing?

And how can it mess up our lives?

What are Quantum Computers?

...and how do they work?

Quantum computers are computers that utilize a new type of computing - instead of using bits (0s or 1s) like traditional computers do to operate, they use qubits, relying on quantum mechanical principles to operate much faster (faster in the sense that 10 qubits are much more powerful than 10 bits).

Why does society need even faster computers?

...and what can we do with them?

In each passing day, 2.5EB of data is produced on the internet (that's 1000000000000000000 Bytes, or 1 billion Giga Bytes each day). [Source needed] However, that is nothing compared to the projected amount to be produced each day by 2020 - 463EB. [Source] As our data production, consumption, as want to use that data increases - the world needs increasingly powerful computers to process that data. This large-scale of gargantuan amounts of data can be extremely useful in fields such as medicine, mathematics, and theoretical physics. But the advent of Quantum Computing may not be all beneficial.

Will Quantum Computing Spell the End for Security?

...and why does data need to be secure?

All those trillions upon trillions of bytes of data are not just sitting there on someone's computer - they are actively being sent across the internet - and hackers can steal that data. Credit card information, passwords, and any sort of information one might want confidential are vulnerable. In order to prevent this, data is scrambled according to an algorithm (encryption) before being sent over the internet, to be reassembled by the receiving computer (decryption).

While this faster computation can be extremely useful in fields such as medicine, mathematics, and theoretical physics, it will also be able to easily crack the modern encryption standard - 2048 bit RSA encryption (How).
